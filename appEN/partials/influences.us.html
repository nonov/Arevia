Coach & Team

The Coach & Team (CT) movement was developed over thirty years ago by Vincent Lenhardt and 10 schools based on several principles:

The client is considered in his/her totality (professionally, managerially, personally, physically, and emotionally).

The coached person represents the organization. Each part carries the all and is shaped like it (holomorphic principle).

The coach understands the world in his complex thinking (cf Morin), not in claiming to understand everything, but rather in acceptance of its ambiguities and paradoxes.

He focuses on the development of the meaning, autonomy, auto-organization of people and systems in collective intelligence surrounding a shared vision.

The CT coach accompanies its clients in their identity development by helping each person to construct his/her own identity without providing a one-stop solution.

Bibliography :

  • Oser la confiance — B. Martin, V. Lenhardt.

  • Responsables, porteurs de sens — V. Lenhardt.

  • Le métier de coach — F. Délivré.

  • L'intelligence collective en action — V. Lenhardt, P. Bernard.

NLP

The body and mind interact, they form a system.

"We can't not communicate."
Paul Watzlawick.

Communicating is not only speaking. Remember that verbal communication is only 7% of all communication. The silence is a message that we send or receive.

What you say about an object is not the object. It is merely a description of the representation that you have made about it based on your perception of it.

If a part of us have an inappropriate behavior, it acts in the way to satisfy something important for us.

The result can be hateful to others: our unconscious possesses the useful and necessary resources for change.

Transactional Analysis

Transactional Analysis uses a model of the ego into three parts: the ego states diagram which helps to understand how people are structured psychologically, how people operate and express their personality.

It offers a theory of psychic development of the human being: the concept of life script explains how patterns of behavior in our present life which originated in our childhood, continue to grow and strengthen, to reach a goal that we set, goal that escapes most often in our consciousness.

The Transactional analysis is a theory of communication: through the analysis of transactions (verbal or non-verbal exchanges between people), psychological games ...

It provides a method of analyzing systems and organizations: organizational theory (TOB).

The ego states:

A state of ego is a coherent system of thoughts, emotions, and associated behaviors. From the standpoint of the human structure, the AT distinguishes three types of ego states.

The Parent correspond to thoughts, emotions, and behaviors of a person she adopted as its own by mimicking of how their parents (or other parental figures) acted of parental or educational figures.

The Adult characterizes the emotions, thoughts and behaviors that are congruent with the reality of the here and now.

The Child corresponds to thoughts, emotions, and behaviors that are a revival of our own childhood.

Art
